Problem Statement


While 30% of Americans try to avoid gluten-rich foods, many challenges arise in the availability of gluten-free and the price of gluten-free food. Furthermore, some claimed to be gluten-free foods actually contain gluten.

Need statement


People of the Gluten Free community need equal access to gluten-free foods as ordinary people have access to gluten foods to live comfortably.





Prices of gluten-free foods can be overpriced compared to their alternatives. An article by Mahir Turhan, a professor of the British University of Nicosia states that “The disproportionally high prices of GFFs(gluten free foods) is a leading and common issue and alone aggravates other GFF-issues”
The same article Further highlights public and government opinion on gluten free foods even going so far as to label them as an "economic burden". This shows how the wellbeing of gluten-free people or those following a gluten free lifestyle are valued lower than the wellbeing of ordinary people.
The price of a product depends on its supply and demand. Compared to “normal” grain products containing gluten produced for the wider population, the market for gluten-free dietary products is small.
